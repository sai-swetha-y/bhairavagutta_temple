Bhairavagutta Temple

Divine Blessings Always

-

This sacred kshetram stands as the private and devoted spiritual property of Sri M. Kalyana Ramakrishna, Advocate, whose unwavering dedication has preserved and enriched its divine essence.

At this revered site, Sri Maha Kala Bhairava Swamy manifests as a Swayambhu (self-manifested deity), emerging naturally upon a sacred rock beneath the ancient Banyan tree. The hill itself, known as Bhairava Gutta, bears the divine form of a Shiva Linga Aakara, embodying profound spiritual symbolism. According to longstanding local tradition, this sanctified place has been venerated for over three centuries, carrying a legacy of deep-rooted devotion.

In a significant spiritual milestone, Sri M. Kalyana Ramakrishna and his family consecrated a magnificent six-foot deity of Lord Sri Shaneeswara Swamy on 27th February 2003, an auspicious Thursday marked by Ekadasi in the Chitrabhanu Nama Samvatsara. Shortly thereafter, on 1st March 2003, the first Tailabhishekam was performed on an exceptionally rare and sacred day—one that uniquely encompassed Shani Trayodashi during the first half and Maha Shivaratri thereafter. This rare confluence of divine timings renders the occasion extraordinarily auspicious.

This kshetram is revered as a truly unique spiritual sanctuary, distinguished by the profound Guru–disciple tradition it embodies. Here, Sri Maha Kala Bhairava Swamy, the eternal Guru, is worshipped alongside His disciple, Lord Shani, enshrined as Sri Abhaya Shaneeswara Swamy, symbolizing protection, grace, and surrender at the Guru’s feet. The presence of Sri Vinayaka Swamy and Sri Nagendra Swamy further enhances the sanctity of this divine abode.

The temple surroundings flourish as a sacred ecological haven, home to a rich variety of spiritually significant and medicinal flora. Revered species such as Rudraksha, Naga Lingam, Srigandham, Nagamalli, Tani, Krishna Marri (Ficus Krishna), Maredu, Banda Nuvvu, Jammi, Ravi, Vepa, along with sacred Patri used in Vinayaka Chavithi rituals, are abundantly present. Carefully nurtured by the owner, this verdant landscape has evolved into a mini forest of over 10,000 plants, contributing to both ecological balance and spiritual harmony.

Blessed with pristine surroundings, the kshetram offers a pollution-free environment, enriched with high oxygen levels and imbued with profound positive energy and divine vibrations. It stands not merely as a place of worship, but as a serene sanctuary where nature and spirituality converge in perfect harmony.

Sri Maha Kala Bhairava Swamy

The Swayambhu deity and presiding Guru of the kshetram, embodying protection, time, and divine justice.

Sri Abhaya Shaneeswara Swamy

The disciple of Bhairava Swamy, bestowing courage, protection, and relief from planetary hardships.

Sri Vinayaka Swamy

The remover of obstacles and the deity of beginnings, guiding devotees towards success and wisdom.

Sri Nagendra Swamy

The serpent deity symbolizing protection, fertility, and harmony with nature and cosmic energies.
